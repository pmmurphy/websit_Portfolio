  • Meet the Frogs

The Panamanian Golden Frog is the national symbol of Panama. It is named for its bright coloring and lives in the lowland level of the rainforest.

The Lemur Leaf Frog is named for its large reflective eyes similar to those of lemurs. It is native to Panama and its populations have been steadily declining the over the past few years.

The Pirri Range Stub Foot Toad is native to Panama and Columbia. Males and females are brown with small yellow spots. They are usually found in Mountain Forest habitats.

The Harlequin or Clown Frog is named for its bright color, usually yellow, green or red. This frog is native to Panama and Costa Rica and is almost extinct due to its high susceptibilty to the chytrid fungus.

The Spiny-Headed Tree Frog is named for the small pointed projections on its head and dorsal surface. It can be found in the cloud forests of Mexico, Honduras, Costa Rica and Panama.

The Limosa Stubfoot Toad or Clown Frog is black with green spotting. This species is diurnal and lives in the primary rainforest and lowland habitats.
